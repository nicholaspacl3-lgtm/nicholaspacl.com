A little more about me

 My journey moves from rural Texas dirt roads to Australian coastlines, from the music pulse of Austin to the night markets of Taiwan—each place shaping how I design, write, and live.

Who I am

I’m an enthusiastic creative who brings unique problem-solving perspectives. I value relationships very highly, and enjoy casually making music, videos, writing, and learning languages. When I’m not tinkering with digital tools and hardware, you’ll find me on long runs or playing pickup basketball. 

Values

I value Integrity, Initiative, Trust, Problem-solving, Curiosity, Gratitude, and Self-discipline

Strengths

  1. Roots in rural Texas

    Tap to expand

    I grew up in Boerne, Texas with two older brothers and an older sister on ten acres that encouraged creativity and independence. Early on, I found my entrepreneurial spark—making cedar walking sticks, organizing neighborhood bike events, and learning by doing.

  2. Moving to Australia

    Tap to expand

    My family lived in Canberra, Australia for several years, where I grew up alongside classmates from around the world. Between longboarding the city, organizing a skate jam, playing saxophone in jazz and school bands, and exploring places like the Great Barrier Reef, Uluru, and the Daintree Rainforest, my world expanded quickly.

  3. Growing up between cultures

    Tap to expand

    In the years between Australia, I lived in Northwest Arkansas, immersed in nature and hands-on projects. I spent my time mountain biking, selling duct-tape wallets and accessories, and renovating a boat alongside my dad.

  4. Coming of age in Austin, TX

    Tap to expand

    Austin became a grounding chapter where I played tennis at Westwood High School and absorbed the city’s live music culture. I later graduated from Texas State University while balancing leadership as a fraternity president and experience as an education consultancy intern. During this time, I also helped scale operations and develop a strong team at Austin’s flagship Gyu-Kaku Japanese BBQ location, gaining hands-on experience in leadership and growth.

  5. Moving to Taiwan

    Tap to expand

    I moved to Taiwan to challenge myself and deepen my cultural understanding after befriending a kind auntie near the Texas State campus. While exploring the island, teaching English, and transitioning into wireless testing, a tech event sparked my interest in edge AI and led me to begin studies at NCCU.

  6. Current endeavors

    Open

    Today, I split my time between Taiwan and the United States, continuing to learn across cultures and disciplines. I’m open to global opportunities that align with my values, curiosity, and long-term ambitions.
